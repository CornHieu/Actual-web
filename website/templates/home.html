1.Introduction

After two months of grueling preparation for the SAT and bringing back home a decent result, I was left with like a month or two of summer break with pretty much nothing to do and no idea to use the spare time for the good. But fast forward a month and a half, here i am with a sleeky designed blog that I can be proud of ðŸ˜Ž

The motivation,initially, sparks from my curiosity regarding all the new techs that have been all-the-rage out there. My ambitious mind was sprawling with bunch of crazy ideas like trying to build an actual SaaS product or data monetization from web scraping. But before touching on those things, I wanted to branch out my skills first by trying out simple stuffs, which is building a blog!

It may sounds boring at first but i think this project is generally a good starting point for me. It will be a solid proof for my passion and the springboard for all the exciting upcoming things. I was able to get my hands on the basics of web development and how the front-end and back-end are interconnected. Not only that, this blog will be the place where I document future projects and updates about my life because I love sharing my personal experiences.

2. A little bit of what I have learned along the way

A scene from Oppenheimer

When it comes to web development, the first things that comes to me and people's mind are HTML and CSS. So I taught myself those two via The Odin Project . After that, with prior experiences in Python, I learned how to set up Flask for the back-end and url handlings for the front-end. Throughout the experimental phase with HTML and CSS, I found out that using CSS alone for design is too much of a hassle so I complemented the website with BootStrap (which I later regretted as Tailwind CSS is a much better option).

With all that, I got myself a simple page that can navigate around. But the exciting part is when I decided to create the homepage. Drawing inspirations from various sources, I came up with the ideas of implementing animations for the homepage, which requires some time for me to get comfortable with Javascript, React, and how Webpack bundling all works together. In those time, I stumbled across a multitude of new concepts, especially how hooks in React works so that I can apply animations with some magic from Framer motion and SVGs.

Putting all the front-end asides, I also created the comment section below this post as a challenge. This has introduced me to FireBase as a solution to manage database and queries.

3. Conclusion

Throughout this journey, surely there are times when I just want to give up and yield myself to the frustrations from many weird bugs that even Google don't have an answer to. Howevever, there is also one thing for sure is that I have stepped foot in the world of web development and left an unique mark there. After this blog, I will be continuing my journey but this time the projects will be larger in scale and much more impactful that can solve actual problems.
